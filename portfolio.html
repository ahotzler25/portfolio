Lost in the Shadows Between Worlds

Master of Arts, Creative Thesis: Bemidji State University (Written in Twine - SugarCube 2)

"Lost in the Shadows Between Worlds" is a choice-based branching narrative that follows a young father struggling to maintain composure and himself beside his teenage daughter's hospital bed as he fights through his grief, confusion, and anger. Modeled after Ernest Hemingway's 1927 short story "Hills Like White Elephants" and stylistically inspired by Cormac McCarthy, Toni Morrison, and Mass Effect, there's an elephant in a room that no one can say; even the hospital staff dance around the word. Each choice causes subtle changes in characterization and reader interpretation, leaving each reading a slightly different experience.

This project successfully (and to great praise) met the Master of Arts, English thesis requirements. Degree awarded May 2023.

The Adventure of Ignatius: Between Two Worlds

GameDev.TV GameJam 2023 Entry (Ten days)

Theme: Life in two dimensions

Play as two characters, one a knight in shining armor, the other a clumsy, awkward Bloo, both trying to make it through trans-dimensional dungeons to escape. Each dungeon is mirrored, a replica of the other in their respective world. Each character quickly learns they're not in this alone. They must work together in order to escape before whatever put them in their dungeons returns. The game was created in 10 days, for the GameDevTV Game Jam 2023.
